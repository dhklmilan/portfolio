How Will the Climate Change Shift the Suitable Area of Seven Woody Bamboo Species in Nepal? Evidences from Ensemble Species Distribution Modeling.

This study examines the distribution of seven woody bamboo species in Nepal:

Bambusa balcooa,
Bambusa jaintiana,
Bambusa nepalensis,
Bambusa nutans,
Bambusa teres,
Dendrocalamus hamiltonii,
Dendrocalamus hookeri

This research will explore how bamboo species are distributed under present and in the future climate condition, informing regional conservation efforts and planning. Occurrence points and predictor variables are the two main datasets needed for modeling. The Forest Research and Training Center provided the occurrence points; while predictor variables were obtained from WorldClim. This study utilized 22 predictor variables (19 bioclimatic, 3 topographic) used to create robust habitat suitability models. A range of machine learning models (MaxEnt, RF, BRT, FDA, SVM, CART, MARS) and statistical models (GLM, GAM) were employed for this analysis using SDM package in Rstudio. To obtained accurate models, only models having (TSS > 0.7 and ROC-AUC > 0.9) were used in the final ensemble, which produced more reliable and precise habita suitability predictions.
