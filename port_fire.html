Mapping the devastated flash flood in Melamchi

Forest fire incidents in the second week of April 2022 showed the worrisome trend of fire hazards in the country, with 3028 incidents of forest fires. In this map, I tried to visualize the spatial pattern of incidents in Nepal during a week at different administrative boundaries at the local, district, and province level. The visualization highlights the pattern of forest fires according to the district.
In 2022, Nepal experienced a significant number of forest fires, particularly during the dry season, which caused extensive damage to both the environment and local communities. The fires mainly affected the mid-hills and the Terai regions, areas prone to seasonal fires due to high temperatures, dry vegetation, and human activities like slash-and-burn farming. The forest fires of 2022 were exacerbated by the country's changing climate, which has led to longer dry spells and higher temperatures, making forests more susceptible to ignition. Strong winds during the fire season further fueled the flames, causing the fires to spread rapidly.
