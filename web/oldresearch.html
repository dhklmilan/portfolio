Forest Fire Risk Assessment using Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis and Earth Observation Technique in Mountain Regions: Insights from Rasuwa District, Nepal
Kunwar, s., Dhakal, M., Silwal, R.
To be submitted to Ecology and Evolution

Rasuwa District, located in Nepal's central Himalayan region, is highly vulnerable to forest fires due to its rugged terrain, dense vegetation, and climatic variability. This research uses remote sensing and GIS to identify fire-prone areas, considering factors such as topography, climatic, anthropogenic, and biophysical. Anthropogenic activities, including proximity to roads and settlements, significantly contribute to fire risks. The findings reveal that lower-altitude areas with high human activity are most susceptible, particularly during dry seasons with prolonged low rainfall. The study highlights the need for early warning systems, community awareness, and sustainable land-use practices to mitigate fire risks. By providing a detailed fire risk map, the research aids policymakers in prioritizing high-risk zones and allocating resources effectively, contributing to forest conservation and community safety.

Research Experiences

Distribution of Red Panda Gaurishankar Conservation Area (GCA)

The Red Panda, a charismatic and endangered species, inhabits the temperate forests of Nepal. This research highlights habitat preferences, threats from habitat fragmentation, and conservation efforts. The study integrates GIS-based mapping and ecological assessments to identify key regions crucial for Red Panda conservation in Gaurishankar Conservation Area (GCA), Nepal.
