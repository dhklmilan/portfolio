FAHP Data Collection: Expert Opinion

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured decision-making method used to analyze complex problems by breaking them down into simpler components. It is one of the most frequently used multi-criteria decision-making methods and is applied in situations where both objective and subjective decisions of decision-makers need to be evaluated together. AHP involves decomposing a problem into a hierarchy of criteria, sub-criteria, and alternatives, allowing decision-makers to evaluate options based on their relative importance. The process differs significantly when judgments are collected in the AHP. To conduct a pairwise comparison matrix, a questionnaire must first be designed and then distributed to experts. Respondents provide responses for each criterion by evaluating pairwise comparisons (e.g., Option A vs. Option B, Option A vs. Option C, etc.). After collecting opinions from multiple experts, their judgments can be aggregated using an averaging method or weighted average method.
I developed the template with major modifications from Template AHP (Nuwan) on the Jotform platform. I attempted to publish the template with 11 factor comparisons, focusing more significantly on its application in the field of GIS-MCDA.
